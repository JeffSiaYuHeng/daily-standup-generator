# ✅ Internship Logbook Writing Format & Rules

## 📌 1. Writing Perspective Rule (VERY IMPORTANT)

* Every sentence should **start with “I” (First-person active voice)**
* This shows **personal involvement, responsibility, and learning**

✅ Correct:

* *I attended a meeting with the Project Manager.*
* *I analyzed the existing dashboard interface.*
* *I updated the project documentation.*

❌ Avoid:

* *A meeting was attended.*
* *The dashboard was analyzed.*
* *Work was done on documentation.*

👉 Always show **you are the doer.**

---

## 📌 2. Standard Flow for Each Daily Entry

Every log entry should follow this **4-Step Execution Flow**

### ⭐ Step 1 — Context / Situation (What happened first)

Explain:

* Where you went
* Who you met
* What task was assigned
* What project you are involved in

Example structure:

> I reported to the Human Resources department for orientation.
> I received a briefing about the company operations.

---

### ⭐ Step 2 — Action / Task Execution (What you actually did)

Explain:

* Tools used
* Process taken
* Design / coding / meeting / research work
* Collaboration

Example:

> I began redesigning the dashboard interface using Figma.
> I analyzed the old interface to understand its structure.

---

### ⭐ Step 3 — Purpose / Objective (Why you did it)

This is VERY important for lecturers.

Explain:

* Goal of the task
* Problem being solved
* Requirement from supervisor

Example:

> I focused on improving usability and maintaining the minimalistic design standard required by the team.

---

### ⭐ Step 4 — Result / Learning Outcome (What happened after)

Explain:

* Progress made
* Submission
* Skills learned
* Feedback received

Example:

> I completed the revised layout and submitted the design assets before the deadline.
> I learned how to structure UI components to make them easier for developers to implement.

---

## 📌 3. Sentence Style Rules

✅ Use:

* Clear
* Formal
* Short-medium length sentences
* Past tense (because logbook records completed work)

Example tense usage:

* I attended
* I discussed
* I designed
* I completed
* I submitted

❌ Avoid:

* Slang
* Emotional language
* Overly long storytelling
* Future tense unless planning

---

## 📌 4. Technical Detail Rule

Include **just enough technical detail** to show:

* You understand the task
* You contributed intellectually

Good examples:

* I adjusted layout spacing and component hierarchy.
* I ensured the documentation visuals matched the current project scope.
* I optimized the interface structure for developer implementation.

---

## 📌 5. Professional Vocabulary Pattern (Useful verbs)

You can reuse these verbs every week:

* I attended
* I assisted
* I collaborated
* I analyzed
* I designed
* I refined
* I updated
* I implemented
* I reviewed
* I submitted
* I documented
* I learned

👉 This makes the log look **structured and mature.**

---

## 📌 6. Ideal Log Entry Length

Per day:

✅ 120 — 180 words
(Enough detail but not too long)

Structure:

* 1 paragraph OR
* 2 short paragraphs (better readability)

---

## 📌 7. Golden Rule ⭐

Every entry must answer:

> **What did I do → Why did I do it → What happened → What did I learn**

If one of these is missing → log becomes weak.
